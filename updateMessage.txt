Picture this: three unified commands for three scopes. User, channel, guild. Clean separation, simple mental model.

**Unified Settings Commands (3.16.0)**

The refactor:
- `/set_user` - your personal preferences
- `/set_channel` - channel-level settings (admin)
- `/set_guild` - guild-wide settings (admin)

User settings (via `/set_user setting:X value:Y`):
- `persona` - your default persona (obi, muppet, chef, teacher, analyst, visionary)

Channel settings (via `/set_channel setting:X value:Y`):
- `verbosity` - concise, normal, detailed
- `persona` - force a persona override (includes clear option)
- `conflict_mediation` - enabled, disabled

Guild settings (via `/set_guild setting:X value:Y`):
- `default_verbosity`, `default_persona`
- `conflict_mediation`, `conflict_sensitivity`, `mediation_cooldown`
- `max_context_messages`, `audio_transcription`, `audio_transcription_mode`
- `audio_transcription_output`, `mention_responses`
- `startup_notification`, `startup_notify_owner_id`, `startup_notify_channel_id`
- `startup_dm_commit_count`, `startup_channel_commit_count`

Persona Resolution Order:
1. Channel persona (if set) - highest priority
2. User preference
3. Guild default
4. Environment variable
5. "obi" fallback

**What changed:**
- `src/commands/slash/persona.rs` - /set_user command
- `src/commands/slash/admin.rs` - /set_channel, /set_guild, validation functions, 29 tests
- `src/command_handler.rs` - unified handlers for all three scopes
- `src/database.rs` - schema migration for channel persona
- Removed: `/set_persona`, `/set_channel_verbosity`, `/set_channel_persona`, `/set_guild_setting`

Three scopes. Three commands. Thats it.

Trust the process.
